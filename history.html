All started with a little backflash

  • 1837: Charles Babbage designs the first mechanical computer, the Analytical Engine.
  • 1937: John Atanasoff and Clifford Berry create the first electronic computer, the Atanasoff-Berry Computer (ABC).
  • 1941: Konrad Zuse creates the first programmable computer, the Z3.
  • 1945: John von Neumann designs the first stored-program computer, the EDVAC.
  • 1951: Grace Hopper invents the first compiler, which translates high-level programming languages into machine code.
  • 1957: Fortran, the first high-level programming language, is developed by John Backus and his team at IBM.
  • 1958: Jack Kilby and Robert Noyce independently invent the integrated circuit, which revolutionizes electronics and enables the development of smaller and more powerful computers.
  • 1964: Douglas Engelbart develops the first computer mouse.
  • 1969: ARPANET, the precursor to the internet, is created.
  • 1971: Intel introduces the first microprocessor, the Intel 4004.
  • 1973: Robert Metcalfe invents Ethernet, a widely used networking technology.
  • 1975: Bill Gates and Paul Allen found Microsoft.
  • 1981: IBM introduces its first personal computer, the IBM PC.
  • 1989: Tim Berners-Lee invents the World Wide Web.
  • 1991: Linus Torvalds creates the first version of the Linux operating system.
  • 1998: Sergey Brin and Larry Page found Google.
  • 2007: Apple releases the first iPhone, sparking the smartphone revolution.
